\chapter{Einleitung}
In the course of increasing digitalization, internet-based platforms and AI-supported information systems have 
become an integral part of everyday life. Social media platforms, search engines, and digital ecosystems such as 
Google, TikTok, Meta, and X process large amounts of user-related data in order to personalize content, optimize 
engagement, and predict user behavior.

Particularly younger generations, who grow up in highly digitalized environments, are increasingly 
exposed to algorithmically controlled recommendation and monitoring systems. Through continuous data collection and 
behavioral analysis, digital platforms are capable of creating detailed user profiles and influencing 
visibility, interaction patterns, and decision-making processes.

At the same time, large-scale monitoring and evaluation systems are also becoming increasingly relevant within 
state-centered digital infrastructures. Especially in the context of China, discussions surrounding social 
scoring systems and digital surveillance demonstrate how extensive data analysis can be connected with 
mechanisms of behavioral regulation and social evaluation.

Against this background, AI-supported monitoring systems represent an increasingly important field within 
digital information systems research. This thesis therefore examines how AI-supported monitoring systems 
function as digital information systems and to what extent they enable data-driven analysis and influence of 
human behavior. Particular focus is placed on data collection processes, algorithmic analysis methods, 
personalization mechanisms, and behavioral influence through digital platforms and monitoring infrastructures
