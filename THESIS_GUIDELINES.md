# 1. General Scientific Principles

Scientific work at FOM must:

- be evidence-based,
- follow a systematic and fact-oriented methodology,
- use appropriate scientific research methods,
- derive methodology from the research question,
- ensure comprehensibility and transferability of results,
- maintain objectivity and neutral language.

The work must demonstrate that the student can independently solve a problem using scientific methods within a limited timeframe.

Key evaluation criteria:

- Scientific rigor
- Methodological appropriateness
- Logical structure
- Neutral argumentation
- Literature quality
- Correct citation style
- Formal correctness
- Practical relevance

Sources:
- FOM Leitfaden 2024
- Vorbereitungsseminar Bachelor Thesis (Prof. Dr. Dieter Litzinger)
- Kurzgutachten Bewertungskriterien

# 3. Required Thesis Structure

## 3.1 Introduction

The introduction must contain:

### Introduction / Context

- Motivation
- Context of the topic
- Research gap
- Practical relevance

### Problem Statement

- Clearly defined research problem
- Research question(s)
- Explanation of why the problem is relevant

### Objective of the Thesis

- Clear objective
- Scope and limitations
- Expected contribution

### Methodological Approach

The methodology must explicitly explain:

- what method is used,
- why this method was selected,
- how the method will be applied.

The procedural flow of the thesis must also be described in prose.

---

## 3.2 Foundations / Theoretical Background

This section should:

- define important concepts,
- explain technological and theoretical foundations,
- provide scientific background literature,
- establish the conceptual basis for later analysis.

For the current thesis topic this includes:

- Artificial Intelligence
- Machine Learning
- Digital Information Systems
- Monitoring Systems
- Algorithmic Governance
- Platform Economies
- Recommender Systems
- Data-driven Decision Systems

---

## 3.3 Main Analysis / Investigation

The main chapters must:

- answer the research question,
- systematically analyze the topic,
- apply scientific literature critically,
- connect technological foundations with socioeconomic implications.

The argumentation must remain:

- neutral,
- evidence-based,
- logically structured,
- scientifically grounded.

---

## 3.4 Conclusion

The conclusion must include:

### Summary of Findings

- concise synthesis of results,
- answer to the research question,
- interpretation of findings.

### Critical Reflection

- limitations of the work,
- methodological limitations,
- possible biases in literature.

### Outlook / Future Research

Only include if new research questions emerge.

---

# 4. Language and Style Requirements

The thesis must:

- use neutral scientific language,
- avoid emotional or political wording,
- avoid colloquial expressions,
- avoid first-person formulations (“I”, “we”),
- avoid rhetorical questions,
- maintain grammatical correctness.

Quotes must use language-appropriate quotation marks:

- German: „...“
- English: “...”

Quotation marks must only be used for actual quotations.

---

# 5. Methodological Requirements

## 5.1 Current Thesis Methodology

The current thesis follows:

- a literature-based,
- conceptual-analytical,
- non-empirical research approach.

No empirical data collection or software development is performed.

The work focuses on:

1. Analysis of technological foundations
2. Identification of structural system characteristics
3. Analysis of socioeconomic implications
4. Systematic categorization and synthesis

---

## 5.2 Literature Review Requirements

The literature review must:

- be systematic,
- document databases used,
- document keywords used,
- document selection criteria,
- describe inclusion/exclusion logic,
- allow replicability.

The literature search process itself is part of scientific rigor.

The following must be explicitly documented:

- databases,
- keywords,
- publication periods,
- filtering logic,
- backward/forward search procedures.

Important references:

- vom Brocke et al.
- Webster & Watson
- Levy & Ellis

---

## 5.3 Qualitative Research Considerations

Even though the thesis is literature-based, the following principles apply:

- systematic categorization,
- transparent analysis procedure,
- intersubjective traceability,
- structured interpretation.

Thematic categorization should follow clear analytical rules.

# 6. Research Questions and Hypotheses

## Central Research Question

How do AI-supported monitoring systems function as digital information systems and which socioeconomic implications are discussed in scientific literature?

---

## Hypotheses

### H1

AI-supported monitoring systems are characterized by multi-layered architectures consisting of:

- data collection,
- analysis,
- evaluation,
- algorithmic selection.

### H2

Machine learning integration creates data-driven decision and evaluation structures that supplement or partially replace traditional control mechanisms.

### H3

Scientific literature attributes significant socioeconomic implications to AI-supported monitoring systems, especially regarding:

- information selection,
- behavioral influence,
- governance structures.

# 9. Formatting and Styling Guidelines

## 9.1 General Formatting

The formatting of the thesis must remain consistent throughout the entire document.

Recommended formatting based on FOM guidelines:

- Font: Times New Roman or Arial
- Font size:
  - Body text: 11 pt or 12 pt
  - Footnotes: 10 pt
- Line spacing:
  - 1.5 lines in body text
  - single-line spacing in footnotes
- Text alignment: justified
- Margins:
  - Left: 3–4 cm
  - Right: 2–2.5 cm
  - Top: 2–2.5 cm
  - Bottom: 2–2.5 cm
- Automatic page numbering required
- Consistent heading hierarchy required

Recommended paragraph formatting:

- No excessive spacing between paragraphs
- Consistent spacing before/after headings
- New sections should begin clearly separated

---

## 9.2 Scientific Writing Style

The thesis should:

- prioritize precision over rhetorical style,
- formulate arguments analytically,
- avoid emotional wording,
- avoid ideological framing,
- distinguish clearly between facts, interpretation, and literature positions.

Preferred style:

- concise sentences,
- technically precise terminology,
- evidence-supported claims,
- structured transitions between sections.

Avoid:

- overly dramatic wording,
- activist language,
- unsupported assumptions,
- broad generalizations.

---

## 9.3 Page Structure

Each chapter should:

- begin with a short introduction,
- maintain logical flow,
- end with a short transition or interim conclusion when appropriate.

Recommended structure within analytical chapters:

1. Introduction of concept
2. Literature overview
3. Critical analysis
4. Comparison or synthesis
5. Transition to next section

---

## 9.4 Citations and Footnotes

Footnotes should:

- remain concise,
- contain supporting references,
- avoid extensive side discussions.

Direct quotations:

- only when necessary,
- must include exact page numbers,
- should not dominate the thesis.

Paraphrasing is generally preferred over excessive direct quotation.

---

## 9.5 Tables and Figures Formatting

Tables and figures must:

- use consistent formatting,
- maintain readable font sizes,
- include captions,
- include source references,
- be referenced in text before appearance.

Captions:

- Tables → caption above table
- Figures → caption below figure

Example:

- Figure 3: Multi-layered Monitoring Architecture
- Source: Own illustration based on Kitchin (2017)

---

## 9.6 Bibliography Formatting

The bibliography must:

- use one consistent citation style,
- maintain alphabetical order,
- include complete bibliographic information,
- contain DOI links where possible.

Missing metadata is considered a formal weakness.

---

## 9.7 AI Usage Documentation

Current FOM guidelines require documentation of AI-generated content.

The thesis should therefore include:

- a KI-Verzeichnis,
- documentation of significant AI-supported work,
- potentially selected prompts in the appendix if required by the supervisor.

AI tools may support:

- language improvement,
- structuring,
- brainstorming,
- summarization.

However, scientific responsibility remains fully with the student.

# 10. Common Mistakes to Avoid

## Methodology Problems

- Missing methodological justification
- Vague research questions
- Lack of analytical structure
- Pure description without analysis

## Literature Problems

- Weak source quality
- Too many internet sources
- Missing peer-reviewed literature
- No documented search strategy

## Language Problems

- Emotional wording
- Political bias
- Informal language
- “I think” formulations

## Structural Problems

- No clear chapter logic
- Missing transition between sections
- Weak conclusion
- No critical reflection

The thesis should:

- remain analytically focused,
- avoid ideological positioning,
- maintain strong scientific rigor,
- document the literature review transparently,
- consistently connect technical system structures with socioeconomic implications,
- emphasize Wirtschaftsinformatik relevance,
